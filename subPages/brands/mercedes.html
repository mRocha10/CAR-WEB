🏛️ Brand History & Legacy

Founded in 1926 through the merger of Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft and Benz & Cie, Mercedes-Benz carries the legacy of Karl Benz, who invented the first gasoline-powered automobile in 1886. The three-pointed star symbolizes the company's ambition to motorize land, sea, and air.

Mercedes-Benz has pioneered countless automotive innovations including the safety cell, crumple zones, ABS brakes, and airbags. The brand's commitment to safety and engineering excellence has made it the benchmark for luxury automobiles worldwide.

Luxury & Performance Excellence

The S-Class has been the flagship of luxury for decades, introducing technologies that eventually trickle down to all vehicles. AMG, Mercedes' performance division, creates some of the world's most powerful and desirable performance cars, from the GT to the Project ONE hypercar.

Today, Mercedes leads luxury electrification with the EQ line, including the revolutionary EQS electric sedan offering over 350 miles of range with uncompromised luxury.
