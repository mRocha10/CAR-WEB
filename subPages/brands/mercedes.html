Mercedes-Benz

The Best or Nothing

Historical Overview

The Birth of Innovation (1886-1926)

Mercedes-Benz's story begins with Karl Benz's patent of the first gasoline-powered car in 1886. Simultaneously, Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach were developing their own motorized vehicles. The merger of Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft and Benz & Cie in 1926 formed Mercedes-Benz, marking the beginning of the world's oldest automotive manufacturer.

Setting Industry Standards (1926-1945)

The company quickly established itself as a pioneer in automotive technology and luxury. The Mercedes-Benz 260 D in 1936 became the world's first diesel-powered passenger car. The iconic 300 SL Gullwing, introduced in 1954, revolutionized sports car design with its distinctive doors and fuel-injected engine.

Post-War Renaissance (1945-1990)

After World War II, Mercedes-Benz led the automotive recovery with innovations in safety technology, including crumple zones and anti-lock braking systems. The S-Class series, introduced in 1972, became the flagship of automotive excellence, introducing numerous technological innovations that later became industry standards.

Modern Era (1990-Present)

Mercedes-Benz continues to pioneer automotive technology with advances in electric mobility, autonomous driving, and digital integration. The launch of Mercedes-EQ marks a new chapter in the company's commitment to sustainable luxury mobility.

Current Brand Focus

Luxury Leadership

Mercedes-Benz maintains its position as the world's leading luxury vehicle manufacturer, focusing on premium quality, comfort, and cutting-edge technology.

Electric Future

Through the Mercedes-EQ sub-brand, the company is aggressively expanding its electric vehicle lineup, aiming for a fully electric portfolio by 2030 where market conditions allow.

Digital Innovation

The MBUX (Mercedes-Benz User Experience) system represents the company's commitment to digital integration and artificial intelligence in automotive technology.

Sustainable Luxury

Mercedes-Benz is pioneering sustainable luxury through innovative materials, carbon-neutral production facilities, and circular economy initiatives.
