Founded in 1903 by Henry Ford, Ford Motor Company revolutionized manufacturing with the assembly line, making automobiles affordable for the average American. The Model T, introduced in 1908, transformed American society by providing reliable, affordable transportation to millions.

Ford's innovation continued with the F-Series trucks, launched in 1948. The F-150 has been America's best-selling vehicle for 46 consecutive years, a testament to its durability, capability, and value. The legendary Mustang, introduced in 1964, created the "pony car" segment and remains an American icon.

Leading the Electric Revolution

Ford is leading the charge into electrification with the groundbreaking F-150 Lightning, bringing electric power to America's best-selling truck, and the Mustang Mach-E, combining Mustang performance with electric efficiency. Ford's commitment to electric vehicles includes $50+ billion investment in EV development.

From inventing mass production to leading the electric revolution, Ford continues to shape the automotive industry while maintaining its core values of durability, capability, and American ingenuity.
