Ford

Built Ford Tough

Historical Overview

The Birth of Modern Manufacturing (1903-1927)

Henry Ford founded the Ford Motor Company in 1903, revolutionizing the automotive industry with the introduction of the assembly line in 1913. The Model T, introduced in 1908, made automobiles accessible to the average American, selling over 15 million units by 1927. Ford's innovative $5 workday and profit-sharing program set new standards for industrial labor relations.

Innovation and Expansion (1927-1945)

The introduction of the Model A in 1927 marked Ford's evolution toward more sophisticated vehicles. The company expanded globally and introduced the first Mercury vehicles in 1939. During World War II, Ford's massive production capabilities were crucial to the Allied war effort, producing bombers, tanks, and other military vehicles.

Post-War Growth (1945-1990)

Ford led post-war innovation with vehicles like the Thunderbird (1955) and Mustang (1964), creating the 'pony car' segment. The F-Series pickup truck, introduced in 1948, became America's best-selling vehicle. The company acquired luxury brands like Jaguar and Volvo, expanding its global presence.

Modern Era (1990-Present)

Ford has embraced sustainability and technology, introducing hybrid vehicles like the Escape Hybrid in 2004. The company has focused on truck and SUV leadership while investing in electric vehicles, exemplified by the Mustang Mach-E and F-150 Lightning. Ford's commitment to innovation continues with advances in connectivity and autonomous driving technology.

Current Brand Focus

Electrification

Ford is investing heavily in electric vehicles, with the F-150 Lightning and Mustang Mach-E leading the charge toward a sustainable future.

Truck Leadership

Maintaining dominance in the pickup truck segment with continuous innovation in the F-Series lineup.

Connected Services

Ford's SYNC technology and FordPass connect vehicles with digital services for enhanced user experience.

Performance Heritage

Continuing the legacy of performance vehicles through Ford Performance and iconic models like the Mustang.
