Jeep

Go Anywhere, Do Anything

Brand History

Born in 1941 as a military vehicle during World War II, Jeep has evolved into a symbol of freedom and adventure. The original Willys MB, known as the Jeep, proved so versatile and reliable that it became the foundation for a new category of civilian vehicles, leading to the creation of the modern SUV segment.

After the war, Jeep transformed its military success into civilian appeal, introducing iconic models like the CJ (Civilian Jeep) series and the Wagoneer, which pioneered the luxury SUV segment. Throughout its history, Jeep has maintained its commitment to exceptional off-road capability while expanding its appeal to everyday drivers.

Current Brand Focus

Today, Jeep continues to lead in off-road capability while embracing modern technology and sustainability. The brand's key priorities include:

  • Electrification: Introducing 4xe plug-in hybrid technology across the lineup
  • Trail Rated: Maintaining superior off-road capabilities
  • Modern Technology: Integrating advanced safety and connectivity features
  • Global Expansion: Developing vehicles for diverse international markets
