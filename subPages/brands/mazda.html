Mazda

Feel Alive

Brand History

Founded in 1920 as the Toyo Cork Kogyo Co., Ltd., Mazda began as a manufacturer of cork products before transitioning to machine tools and vehicles. The company's first passenger car, the R360 Coupe, was introduced in 1960, marking the beginning of Mazda's journey in automotive innovation.

Mazda made history with the development of the rotary engine, becoming the only manufacturer to successfully mass-produce this unique powerplant. The iconic RX-7 and RX-8 sports cars showcased this technology, while the MX-5 Miata redefined the affordable sports car segment, becoming the best-selling two-seat convertible in history.

Current Brand Focus

Today, Mazda positions itself as a premium mainstream brand, focusing on sophisticated design and engaging driving dynamics. The company's key priorities include:

  • SKYACTIV Technology: Advancing efficient powertrains and lightweight engineering
  • Kodo Design: Evolving the "Soul of Motion" design philosophy
  • Premium Experience: Elevating the brand with upscale materials and craftsmanship
  • Driving Dynamics: Maintaining the Jinba Ittai (horse and rider as one) philosophy
