Toyota

Let's Go Places

Brand History

Founded in 1937 by Kiichiro Toyoda, Toyota Motor Corporation has grown from a Japanese textile machinery company into the world's largest automaker. The company's success is built on the foundations of the Toyota Production System, which revolutionized manufacturing with its focus on efficiency, quality, and continuous improvement.

Toyota made history in 1997 with the introduction of the Prius, the world's first mass-produced hybrid vehicle. This pioneering move established Toyota as a leader in sustainable mobility and demonstrated the company's commitment to environmental responsibility.

Current Brand Focus

Toyota continues to lead the automotive industry with its focus on sustainable mobility and technological innovation. The company's key priorities include:

  • Environmental Leadership: Expanding hybrid and hydrogen fuel cell technology
  • Safety Innovation: Developing advanced driver assistance systems
  • Connected Mobility: Enhancing vehicle connectivity and smart features
  • Quality Assurance: Maintaining Toyota's legendary reliability standards
