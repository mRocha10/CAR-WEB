Brand History

Founded in 1911 by Louis Chevrolet and William C. Durant, Chevrolet has grown to become one of America's most iconic automotive brands. The company quickly established itself as a major force in the automotive industry by offering reliable, affordable vehicles that appealed to a broad range of consumers.

Throughout its history, Chevrolet has produced numerous legendary vehicles, from the revolutionary Small Block V8 engine in 1955 to the iconic Corvette, which has represented American performance for over 65 years. The brand's commitment to innovation and value has made it a cornerstone of General Motors and American automotive culture.

Current Brand Focus

Today, Chevrolet continues to innovate across all vehicle segments while embracing an electric future. The brand's key priorities include:

  • Electric Innovation: Expanding EV offerings with models like the Bolt EV and upcoming electric trucks
  • Performance Heritage: Continuing the Corvette and Camaro legacy
  • Truck Excellence: Advancing the Silverado lineup with new technology
  • Safety Technology: Developing advanced driver assistance systems
