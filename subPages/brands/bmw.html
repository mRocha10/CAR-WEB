üèõÔ∏è Brand History & Legacy

Founded in 1916 as Bayerische Motoren Werke (BMW), the company began as an aircraft engine manufacturer before transitioning to motorcycles in 1923 and automobiles in 1928. The iconic BMW roundel logo represents a spinning aircraft propeller, paying homage to the company's aviation heritage.

BMW has built its reputation on delivering the "Ultimate Driving Machine" - vehicles that perfectly blend performance, luxury, and driving dynamics. From the legendary BMW 328 sports car of the 1930s to the revolutionary M3 and the groundbreaking i3 electric vehicle, BMW has consistently pushed the boundaries of automotive engineering.

Engineering Excellence & Motorsport Heritage

BMW's commitment to performance is deeply rooted in motorsport. The BMW M Division, founded in 1972, has created some of the world's most celebrated performance cars. From dominating Formula 1 to winning 24 Hours of Le Mans, BMW's racing success directly influences its road cars, delivering technologies and innovations that enhance the driving experience.

Today, BMW stands at the forefront of electrification with its BMW i and iX series, combining sustainable mobility with the brand's signature driving dynamics. The company's vision of "Freude am Fahren" (The Joy of Driving) continues to define every vehicle it creates.
