BMW

The Ultimate Driving Machine

Historical Overview

Origins and Early Years (1916-1945)

BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke) was founded in 1916, initially producing aircraft engines. The company's iconic blue and white roundel logo represents spinning propeller blades against a blue sky. After World War I, BMW shifted to motorcycle production in 1923 and began automobile production in 1928 with the acquisition of Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach.

Post-War Recovery and Growth (1945-1970)

After World War II, BMW rebuilt from near destruction, starting with motorcycle production. The launch of the BMW 501 luxury car in 1951 marked its return to automobile manufacturing. The introduction of the Isetta microcar helped the company survive financial challenges. The revolutionary BMW New Class sedans, beginning with the 1500 in 1961, established BMW's reputation for sports sedans.

The Modern Era (1970-2000)

BMW launched its performance-oriented M division in 1972. The first 3 Series debuted in 1975, establishing the foundation for BMW's core model range. The company acquired the Rover Group in 1994, though later divested most of it while retaining MINI. The launch of the X5 in 1999 marked BMW's entry into the SUV market.

21st Century Innovation (2000-Present)

BMW has embraced sustainable mobility with the launch of the i sub-brand in 2011, pioneering electric vehicles like the i3 and i8. The company continues to expand its electrified lineup while maintaining its commitment to driving dynamics and luxury.

Current Brand Focus

Driving Dynamics

BMW maintains its core identity as the Ultimate Driving Machine, focusing on performance, handling, and driver engagement across all models.

Electrification

Through BMW i, the company is expanding its electric vehicle range with models like the i4 and iX, combining sustainability with signature driving dynamics.

Digital Integration

BMW's iDrive system and digital services showcase the brand's commitment to connectivity and user experience innovation.

Luxury Innovation

BMW continues to push boundaries in luxury design and materials, while incorporating advanced technology and sustainability.
