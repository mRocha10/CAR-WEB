Founded in 1948 by Soichiro Honda, Honda Motor Co. began as a motorcycle manufacturer before entering automobile production in 1963. Honda's founder instilled a philosophy of innovation, quality, and challenging conventions that continues to define the brand today.

Honda revolutionized the automotive industry with the introduction of VTEC (Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control) technology in 1989, delivering both performance and fuel efficiency. The Honda Civic, introduced in 1972, became one of the best-selling cars globally, known for reliability, fuel economy, and practicality.

Engineering Excellence

Honda's commitment to engineering excellence is evident in every vehicle. The Honda Accord has been America's best-selling car for decades, while the CR-V pioneered the compact SUV segment. Honda's reputation for building vehicles that last 200,000+ miles with minimal issues is unmatched in the industry.

Today, Honda leads in hybrid technology with models like the Accord Hybrid and CR-V Hybrid, while developing advanced safety systems through Honda Sensing technology.
